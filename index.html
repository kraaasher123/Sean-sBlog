The Actual Blog Post

The Topic for the Blog Post will be about "Speedcubing".

First of all, What is Speedcubing? Speedcubing is a sport in which you have to solve a Rubik's Cube as fast as you can. So it's kind of like speedrunning but with Rubik's Cubes.

You might be wondering, "There should be a world record right?".

Yes! There definitely is! The World Record in solving the Rubik's Cube (as of now) is 3.47 seconds, set by Chinese Speedcuber Yu Sheng Du

Where are the past world record times stored? They are stored in the World Cube Association.

What is the World Cube Association? The World Cube Association governs competitions for mechanical puzzles that are operated by twisting groups of pieces, commonly known as 'twisty puzzles'.

So, How do you learn to speedcube? First of all, you have to learn a method to solve it. the most popular method for beginners is called the "Layer by Layer" method.

But, Layer by Layer is slow, that is why there are methods invented specifically for speedcubing.

The first and probably the most popular method is CFOP. CFOP stands for Cross, F2L (First Two Layers), OLL (Orient Last Layer), and PLL (Permute Last Layer)

The second method is called the Roux Method. It is a method in which you have to start by building the First Block, Second Block, CMLL (Corners of the Last Layer), and finally the LSE (Last Six Edges)

The third method is called ZZ short for Zbigniew Zbrowoski (the inventor of the method). This is one of the more hard-to-understand methods, ZBLL (Zbrowoski Last Layer) consists of a whopping 493 Algorithms! That's alot!

There are so many methods in speedcubing that I could cover but I should mention these methods, Old Pochmann, CFCE, and Edges First

Tips and Advice when starting out on Speedcubing

1. Get a Speedcube, you should not use the regular Rubik's Cubes since they are slower and not optimized for speedcubing. I would recommend getting the "RS3M 2021 Maglev", the "Qiyi Mofangge ThunderClap V3M", or the "Meilong 3M"

2. Well obviously, you have to learn a method. I would recommend either CFOP or ROUX

3. Practice makes perfect, it's really important for your speedcubing journey

4. Watch youtube tutorials, that's how I learned to speedcube

5. My final tip, is to enjoy cubing. You can never get good at something you don't enjoy

Well, that's all my tips. I should mention these youtube channels that could help you on your speedcubing journey

  • JPerm
  • BrodyTheCuber
  • CubeSkills

So, Why start Speedcubing? Well, speedcubing is really fun and addictive, you can never let go of the cube. Another reason is bragging rights. Really, the only reason I started speedcubing was because I wanted to flex my fast times. It can also help with your fine motor skills by using your fingers. There's a whole documentary on speedcubing really recommend checking it out.

I think this is pretty much it for this blog post, pretty fun to make. Oh and if you happen to start cubing then I only have two words for you:

Have Fun
